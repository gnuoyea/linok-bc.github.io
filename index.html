Jason Ken Adhinarta

I am a third-year undergraduate at Boston College studying Computer Science and Mathematics. I work as a research assistant at Prof. Donglai Wei’s Computer Vision lab, focusing on the area of connectomics.

I lead the BC Machine Intelligence Group (club Discord), am a member of the BC Experimental Math and Machine Learning Lab, and compete as part of the BC Competitive Programming Team.

Previously, I held a research internship at EPFL’s CVLab where I had the privilege of being mentored by Dr. Jiancheng Yang and Prof. Pascal Fua. Additionally, I interned at Emmerich Research Center under Dr. Eden Steven.

ChunkPipeline
repo

Created a Dask-powered framework for orchestrating data processing pipelines on massive-scale connectomics datasets; ported 3D algorithms (connected components, euclidean distance transform, and TEASAR skeletonization) from the Seung Lab to be chunking-compatible, allowing for efficient utilization of the Boston College Linux Cluster

Palm oil fresh-fruit-bunch grading (2020)

Black soldier fly instance segmentation (2020)

Built an Arduino-based automated data collection system for larvae, used Mask R-CNN and YOLACT++ for instance segmentation; adapted DCNv2 to be compatible with NVIDIA’s APEX for memory-efficient training. Part of a larger project to utilize BSF larvae for waste management
